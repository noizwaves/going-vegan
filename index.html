Simple Muesli Breakfast Template

Great nutritious breakfast. Mix any kinds of grains. Add other ingredients like shredded coconut, dried appricots, berries, almond slivers, etc for variety.
  • 90g of rolled grains (any mix of oats, barley, rye) from Friends of the Earth
  • 20g of ground linseed from Friends of the Earth
  • 10g of bran (wheat, oat, rice) from Friends of the Earth
  • ~200ml of vegan milk (organic soy, almond, oat, rice) from ALDI, Coles, or Woolworths
  • 1 thinly sliced banana from Queen Victoria Markets
  • 1 roughly cut up kiwi fruit from Queen Victoria Markets
  • Splash of coconut yogurt (homemade) or use coconut cream

Simple Steamed Dinner Template

Simple, no thrills dinner of steamed veges, beans (or tofu, tempeh, sprouts), and rice. No oil is used for cooking. Makes 2 servings.
  • 250g of firm tofu (cut into cubes), tempeh (cut into cubes), cooked chickpeas, sprouted lentils (or anything else beany) and marinated in sauce (see below)
  • 2 to 4 colourful vegetables, cut into medium size pieces. Select from broccoli, bok choi, chinese broccoli, mustard greens, garlic shoots, spinach, spinach leaves, capcicum (green, red, or yellow), eggplant, zucchini, mushroom, sweet potato, beetroot, etc etc.
  • 120g of cooked brown rice
For the sauce, some combination to desired consistency and strength of:
  • Water, vinegar, apple cider vinegar
  • Garlic, ginger, cayenne pepper, chilli flakes
  • Tahini
  • Soy sauce
Method:
  1. To make sauce, mix sauce ingredients in container. Mix in beans, shake around to mix, and let marinate for as long as you can
  2. Steam veges for appropriate amount of time (3-10 minutes depending on vegetable)
  3. Transfer veges to saucepan and mix through marinated beans
  4. Serve over heated rice
  5. Top with nutritional yeast

Resturants & Eating Out

  • Meal ideas for eating out (on Pinterest)
  • Melbourne resturants that are vegan/vegetarian, do have vegan options, or can be customised to be vegan (on Foursquare)

Surviving Lunchtimes

  • Sushi rolls: bvegetarian, avocado, or seaweed rolls. Ask if they are out, they will make you fresh rolls!
  • Grill'd vegetarian burger, without cheese or mayo. Ask for something substituted instead :)
  • Mad Mex burrito without sour cream or cheese. Again, ask for it to be substituted with avocado so you don't lose value.
  • Subway vegetarian sub
  • Various Indian resturants (Gopals, Om Vegetarian, Hare Chrishna) along Swanston Street

Surviving Drunk Nights Out

  • Lord of the Fries: chips, onion rings, burgers, hot dogs. Make sure you pick a vegan sauce.
  • Felafel Kebab. For any kebab place. Ask for no cheese, and hummus instead of creamy sauce.
  • Anything vegetarian and fried from China Bar.
  • Hot chips or wedges.

Books

  • The China Study. Comprehensive comparison of asian and western diets, the diffence in animal product intake, and the effect on chronic disease.
  • Eat and Run (by Scott Jurek)
  • Finding Ultra: Rejecting Middle Age, Becoming One of the World's Fittest Men, and Discovering Myself
